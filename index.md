## Curatorial Statement
This digital archive, titled "Top-Selling Shoes from the Three Major Sportswear Brands (2000-2026)," collects landmark best-selling shoe models from PUMA, Nike, and Adidas over the past 26 years. We aim to reflect the transformations in the fashion industry's business models through a material culture lens.

Data collection uses multi-dimensional criteria: online, we cross-validated based on StockX trading volume, brand official reissue records, and rankings from authoritative fashion media such as Hypebeast and Highsnobiety, selecting shoe models with "cultural penetration" in each era—such as the Nike Roshe Run during the Normcore era. Offline, our team conducted on-site visits to Trendy Zone Vintage Shop and found discontinued items like the Nike Prep Low Profile. Additionally, members personally purchased items such as the Adidas Samba, which were also added to our digital archive. Each entry includes release year, technical features, and marketing narratives to form a quantifiable fashion history map.

The significance of this archive is revealing sneakers as "wearable commercial texts": from the silver-tech aesthetic of the 2000s to the minimalist craze of the 2010s, and then to Y2K revival of the 2020s. The cycle of shoe models directly correspond to brands' strategic shift from "selling products" to "selling cultural symbols." These bestsellers are the key to deciphering people’s changing consumption psychology. Also, they reflect critical perspectives—for instance, whether the fashion cycle's revival of old shoes signals creative exhaustion among the three major brands. As these three sportswear gets transition from pure athletic wear to fashion labels, have they overlooked issues , like wearing comfort and inflated pricing.

The challenges during the process include the opacity of brand historical archives, such as the lack of official documentation for early shoe models. There is also controversy over the timeliness of defining bestsellers. It is difficult to balance commercial sales with cultural influence quantitatively. Additionally, the fragmented copyright of visual materials poses an issue. Through a triangular verification method involving vintage physical images, brand museum archives, and streetwear photography databases, we reconstructed the original appearance of each shoe model as accurately as possible.
